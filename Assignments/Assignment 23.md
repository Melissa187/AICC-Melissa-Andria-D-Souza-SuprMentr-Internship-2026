Assignment Name : Semantic Meaning
Description : Find 5 word pairs and explain semantic similarity.

1. Car & Automobile
Relationship: Near-Synonymy (Identity)

Explanation: These words share a high degree of semantic similarity because they refer to the same physical object. In most contexts, they are interchangeable. Their similarity is rooted in denotation—they point to the exact same "referent" in the real world.

2. Ocean & Sea
Relationship: Overlapping Taxonomic Similarity

Explanation: While not identical (an ocean is typically larger and more vast), they share the core semantic features of [+Large], [+Saltwater], and [+Body of water]. They are semantically similar because they belong to the same narrow category of geographical features.

3. Teacher & Professor
Relationship: Hyponymy / Specialized Similarity

Explanation: These words share the semantic "nucleus" of education and instruction. A "professor" is essentially a type of "teacher" within a specific higher-education context. Their similarity is strong because their functional roles in society overlap significantly.

4. Coffee & Tea
Relationship: Co-hyponymy (Coordinate Relationship)

Explanation: These aren't synonyms, but they are semantically similar because they both sit under the "umbrella" of the category Beverages. They share attributes like [+Liquid], [+Caffeinated], and [+Consumed hot or cold]. They are "siblings" in a semantic tree.

5. Laptop & Desktop
Relationship: Functional Similarity

Explanation: These words represent different physical forms of the same concept: a computer. Their similarity is derived from their utility. Even though their physical attributes differ (portable vs. stationary), their semantic purpose—processing data and running software—is identical.
